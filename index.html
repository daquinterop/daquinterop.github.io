About Me

Data Scientist focused on Agriculture and Climate. Specialized in causally-aware methods and process-based modeling to assess the impact of local interventions.

I am PhD researcher in the AI group at Wageningen University. I specialize in bridging the gap between domain-specific process knowledge and machine learning and AI, specifically a field known as Causal ML/AI. By developing causally-aware models, my goal is to provide more reliable, evidence-based support for interventions where traditional correlation-based models fail.

With a background in Agricultural Engineering and Meteorology, I have roughly 8 years of experience leveraging multi-source data to support decision-making in agriculture, food security, and climate. Throughout my career, I have utilized a range of modeling techniques, from transparent process-based models to opaque 'black-box' machine learning. My work spans from developing data-based products for rice farmers in Colombia to building regional yield monitoring systems across Africa and Asia.

In my free time, I enjoy cooking, especially my trademark recipes for lentil soup and beans. I appreciate and advocate for simpler machines, which is why I am very fond of my bicycle. Whenever I have the time, I enjoy researching my genealogy and tracking the steps of my ancestors; it is like solving a puzzle by searching through historical documents and maps.
